Imagine your house. You built it, you live in it, you want to protect it.

Let's look at who's involved:

In preparation: The builder constructed your house. The technician comes regularly for maintenance. An insurance agent sold you home insurance. And perhaps you have a legal advisor for legal matters.

Now the worst happens: A fire.

What happens? The firefighters arrive and put out the flames. The insurance assessor evaluates the damage. If there's a dispute, a lawyer helps you. And in the end, the builder reconstructs.

Now the crucial question: If your house is on fire, do you call the technician who normally services your heating?

No, of course not! The technician has a maintenance contract. He doesn't put out fires. For that, you need the fire department. And for the financial damage, you need insurance.

That's why: Maintenance contract is not equal to insurance is not equal to legal protection. These are three different things. You understand this intuitively for your house.
