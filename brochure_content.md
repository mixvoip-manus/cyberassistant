## 6. Cybercrime & Fraud Protection

From wire transfer fraud to phone system hacking, cybercriminals use sophisticated techniques to steal from businesses. Our coverage protects against financial losses from various forms of cyber fraud.

### Wire Transfer Fraud (CEO Fraud)

Business Email Compromise (BEC) attacks trick employees into transferring funds to criminal accounts. Coverage includes losses from fraudulent wire transfers.

### Identity Theft & Impersonation

When criminals impersonate your company or executives to defraud customers or partners, we cover investigation costs and liability claims.

### Phone System Hacking (Toll Fraud)

VoIP and PBX systems can be compromised to make expensive international calls. Coverage includes fraudulent call charges.

### Additional Coverage

✓ Payment Card Fraud - PCI-DSS fines and card replacement costs
✓ Credential Theft - Account takeover and unauthorized access
✓ Phishing Losses - Financial losses from phishing attacks
✓ Vendor Fraud - Fake invoice and supplier scams

### Example Scenario

**Situation:** An employee receives an urgent email appearing to be from the CEO requesting a €45,000 wire transfer. The email is spoofed and the money goes to criminals.

**Response:** Mixvoip's fraud team works with banks to attempt fund recovery. €12,000 is recovered, and the remaining €33,000 loss is covered by the policy.

## 9. Main Exclusions

Like all insurance policies, Mixvoip Cyber Assistance has certain exclusions. Understanding these helps ensure you have appropriate expectations.

### Not Covered

✗ **Pre-existing incidents:** Cyber events that occurred before the policy start date
✗ **Intentional acts:** Losses caused deliberately by the insured or their employees
✗ **War and terrorism:** State-sponsored attacks or acts of war
✗ **Infrastructure failures:** General internet or power grid outages
✗ **Unpatched systems:** Losses from known vulnerabilities not patched within 30 days
✗ **Cryptocurrency holdings:** Direct theft of cryptocurrency assets
✗ **Bodily injury:** Physical harm resulting from cyber incidents
✗ **Contractual penalties:** Penalties from breach of contract (unless GDPR-related)
✗ **Intellectual property:** Loss of trade secrets or proprietary information value
✗ **Reputational damage:** Long-term brand value diminution

### Conditions & Limitations

**Waiting Periods:** Business interruption: 12 hours | System damage: 24 hours

**Maximum Periods:** Business interruption: 30 days | Extended coverage: 60 days

### ⚠️ Important Note

Maintaining a valid Fit4Cybersecurity assessment and implementing recommended security measures is a condition of coverage. Failure to maintain minimum security standards may result in claim denial.
